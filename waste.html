Waste Management

Waste Segregation Guide

Learn the right way to segregate wet, dry, hazardous, e-waste, and plastic waste. Proper segregation is the first step to a zero-waste campus.

Know Your Bins What Colour Goes Where?
Green Bin
Wet/organic waste
Food scraps, vegetable peels, leaves
Yellow Bin
Dry/recyclable waste
Paper, cardboard, glass, metals
Red Bin
Hazardous waste
Batteries, chemicals, syringes
Blue Bin
E-Waste
Old phones, cables, circuit boards
Grey/Black Bin
Non-recyclable / rejects
Soiled wrappers, thermocol
Wet / Organic Waste
Wet waste is biodegradable and can be composted to create rich manure. It goes in the GREEN bin. Never mix with dry waste it contaminates recyclables!
Fruit & Vegetable Peels
Compost in 24 weeks rich manure
Leftover Food
Can be composted or used for biogas
Dry Leaves & Flowers
Great for garden composting
Tea Bags / Coffee Grounds
Excellent compost material
Coconut Shells
Slow compost chop for faster breakdown
Eggshells
Adds calcium to compost
Garden Trimmings
Mix green and brown materials
Animal/Dairy Waste
Add to compost in small amounts only
Composting
Set up a compost pit or bin in your department garden. Wet waste + dry leaves = rich organic fertiliser in 68 weeks.
Biogas
Our campus biogas plant converts food waste into cooking gas and biofertiliser. 50 kg waste 1 hour of cooking fuel!
Avoid Mixing
Wet + dry waste = contamination. Soiled wet waste cannot be recycled and ends up in landfill. Always segregate at source.
Dry / Recyclable Waste
Dry recyclable waste goes in the YELLOW bin. These materials can be processed and reused saving energy and reducing mining for raw materials.
Newspapers & Magazines
Keep dry can be fully recycled
Cardboard Boxes
Flatten before disposal for space saving
Glass Bottles & Jars
Rinse before recycling. Glass is 100% recyclable.
Metal Cans & Tins
Rinse, crush to save space
Books & Paper
Donate if usable, else recycle
Clean Fabric / Clothes
Donate or send to textile recycler
Clean Plastic Bottles
PET bottles clean, cap off, crush
Shampoo / Soap Bottles
HDPE plastic highly recyclable
Clean Before Recycling
Rinse food residue from containers. Dirty recyclables get rejected at recycling facilities and end up in landfill.
Reuse First
Before recycling, ask: can I reuse this? Glass jars for storage, boxes for packing, paper for notes.
Campus Drop Points
Yellow bins are placed at every building entrance, cafeteria, and hostel block. Find the nearest one on the Campus Map.
Hazardous Waste
Hazardous waste contains chemicals that can harm people and the environment. It MUST go in the RED bin NEVER in general waste or down drains!
Batteries (All Types)
Contain lead, acid. Never in general bin!
CFL / Fluorescent Bulbs
Contain mercury handle carefully
Lab Chemicals
Follow lab disposal protocol strictly
Expired Medicines
Return to pharmacy. Never flush!
Paint & Solvents
Take to hazardous waste collection point
Ink Cartridges
Return to printer shops for refill/recycling
Aerosol Cans
Never puncture take to collection point
Biomedical Waste
Medical facility only strict protocols
Never Mix!
Mixing hazardous waste with regular garbage endangers sanitation workers and contaminates soil and groundwater for years.
Drop-Off Points
Hazardous waste bins are at the Chemistry lab entrance, Workshop, and the main Administrative Block. Check the Campus Map.
Report Violations
See hazardous waste improperly disposed? Report it immediately to the Campus Environmental Officer at ext. 2345.
Electronic Waste (E-Waste)
E-waste is one of the fastest-growing waste streams globally. It goes in the BLUE bin. E-waste contains valuable metals like gold, silver, copper plus toxic materials that must be handled safely.
Old Mobile Phones
Contains gold, copper, rare earth metals
Laptops & Computers
Return to manufacturer or authorised recycler
Cables & Chargers
Copper-rich very recyclable
Monitors & TVs
Old CRTs contain lead special handling needed
Printers & Scanners
Dismantle at authorised facility
Headphones & Speakers
Small but contains recyclable parts
Keyboards & Mouse
Plastic + metal recyclable together
CDs, USBs, Hard Drives
Wipe data first, then e-waste bin
Repair First
The greenest device is one you already have. Campus IT repair centre fixes most issues for free extend device life by 23 years.
Donate
Working old devices can be donated to campus NGO partners for rural education programs. Contact Student Welfare at ext. 2211.
E-Waste Drive
Our monthly E-Waste collection drive happens on the first Saturday of every month at the CSE department entrance.
Plastic-Free Alternatives
The best plastic is the one never used. Here are easy, affordable, and stylish alternatives to common single-use plastics on campus.
Plastic Bottle Steel Bottle
Saves 150+ plastic bottles per year
Plastic Bag Cloth Bag
One cloth bag replaces 700+ plastic bags
Plastic Straw Bamboo Straw
Or just drink without a straw!
Plastic Box Bamboo/Steel Tiffin
Lasts 10+ years vs 1 use
Plastic Cutlery Wooden Cutlery
Biodegrades in months, not centuries
Plastic Wrap Beeswax Wrap
Reusable up to 1 year
Liquid Soap Bottle Soap Bar
Zero packaging waste
Disposable Cup Reusable Mug
Campus canteen gives 5% discount with own cup!
Green Store
Our campus Green Store near the library sells eco-friendly alternatives at subsidised rates for students. Open MonSat, 9am5pm.
The Numbers
One student going plastic-free for a year avoids ~1,460 plastic items (4 per day). Imagine 3,000 students doing the same!
Plastic-Free Badge
Complete 30 days plastic-free and earn the campus Plastic-Free Champion badge, worth 100 eco points on the leaderboard.
